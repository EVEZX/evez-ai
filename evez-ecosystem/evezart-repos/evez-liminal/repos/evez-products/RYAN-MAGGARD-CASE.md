## LEGAL CLAIMS (Multiple)

### 1. Section 1983 — Failure to Protect (Federal Civil Rights)
- **Precedent:** Farmers v. Brennan (1994) — prison officials have duty to protect inmates from violence
- **Standard:** "Deliberate indifference" — they knew or should have known of the risk and failed to act
- **Sock weapons** are well-known in corrections. If the facility didn't prevent this, that's deliberate indifference.
- **Damages:** Compensatory (medical, pain, suffering, future care) + Punitive (to punish the facility)

### 2. Medical Negligence / Deliberate Indifference to Medical Needs
- **Precedent:** Estelle v. Gamble (1976) — deliberate indifference to serious medical needs = 8th Amendment violation
- If brain swelling was not treated promptly and properly, this is a separate federal claim
- **Key question:** How long between assault and medical treatment?

### 3. State Law Claims — Negligence / Personal Injury
- Premises liability (facility failed to maintain safe conditions)
- Negligent supervision (staff didn't monitor inmates)
- Assault and battery (against the attacker)
